About me

Quantum Dynamics of Materials (QUDYMA)

Greetings! I'm Francisco Lobo from Portugal. I was born and raised in Guimarães but later moved to the Invicta city, Porto. I obtained both my bachelor's and master's degree in Physics at the University of Minho in Braga, Portugal. I'm also a vivid enjoyer of Ben 10.

I'm currently a PhD student in the "Quantum Dynamics of Materials" team under the supervision of Pablo San-José at ICMM. I'm working within the field of many-body atosecond physics, devoted to understanding quantum materials and their response when driven out of equilibrium. At the moment, I'm dealing with self-consistent Majorana physics in 1D nanowire systems.

Journal notes

Compilation of notes I wrote on various topics, ranging from simple exercises to detailed state of art calculations. It may be useful to others, but please note that it is not peer-reviewed and will certainly contain errors. Keep here as safekeeping.

Plane wave expansion methods in
2D photonic crystal

Plane wave expansion methods for the determination of the electromagnetic modes and dispersion relations of a 2D photonic crystal.

Dipole line in a laser cavity

Dyadic Green's functions methods to calculate the Purcell factor of a dipole line a laser cavity.

Excitonic properties of hBN

Linear response of an interacting many-body system at 0K from a time-dependent Hartree-Fock mean-field theory as a generalized eigen-problem of the effective two-particle Hamiltonian of the electron-hole interaction.

Hartree-Fock-Nambu mean-field theory

General Hartree-Fock decoupling of a quartic Hamiltonian accounting for superconductivity in a Nambu-spinor scheme. Mean-field self-energy as a spin-1/2 Hubbard term in the Wannier limit.

Minimal Majorana nanowire model

Basic review on bound- and edge-states physics on a 1D semiconducting nanowire in the proximity of a intrinsic superconductor.
